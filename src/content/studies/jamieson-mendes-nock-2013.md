Jamieson, Mendes, and Nock (2013) showed that reappraising physiological stress responses as helpful — rather than trying to calm down — led to improved cardiovascular efficiency and better performance on standardized tests. This "stress-is-enhancing" mindset intervention has been replicated across multiple contexts including math anxiety, social evaluative stress, and public speaking.
