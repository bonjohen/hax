title: "Exercise training increases size of hippocampus and improves memory"

relevance_note: "Landmark RCT showing aerobic exercise increases hippocampal volume by 2% and reverses age-related loss by 1-2 years. 120 older adults, 1-year walking intervention."
evidence_tier: "high"
---

This randomized controlled trial assigned 120 older adults to either an aerobic walking program or a stretching control for one year. The walking group showed a 2% increase in hippocampal volume, effectively reversing age-related volume loss by 1-2 years. The stretching group showed a 1.4% decline. Increased hippocampal volume was associated with higher serum BDNF levels and improved spatial memory performance. This study is one of the strongest demonstrations that moderate aerobic exercise can reverse hippocampal atrophy and improve memory in older adults.
