The original power-posing study by Carney, Cuddy, and Yap (2010) reported that adopting expansive body postures for two minutes led to hormonal changes (increased testosterone, decreased cortisol) and increased risk tolerance. The study has been widely cited but also widely contested, with the lead author (Carney) later distancing herself from the hormonal claims. Replication studies have generally supported the self-reported confidence effects but not the hormonal changes.
